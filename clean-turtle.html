How to Clean Your Turtle

  1. Only bathe your turtle if you notice algae on its shell or if it is shedding dead skin. These are both normal occurrences, but it will be more comfortable if you bathe it. When it is not shedding or growing algae on its shell, it will keep itself clean enough by swimming around in its tank.

  2. Use a plastic washtub that is used only for washing your turtle. Turtles carry salmonella, so you should not wash it in your kitchen or bathroom sink or in any container that you use for anything else. Instead, purchase a dedicated dish pan or bucket.

  3. Fill the washtub with a few inches of lukewarm water that has had the chlorine removed. You can remove the chlorine by filling a pitcher with tap water and letting it sit uncovered for 24 hours. Another method is to boil tap water for 20 minutes, then let it cool until it is lukewarm.

  4. Use a new, soft toothbrush to carefully scrub your turtle’s shell, arms, legs, tail, and plastron (belly). Remember that the shell is sensitive, so use a gentle touch and a soft brush. The rest of your turtle’s body is even more sensitive. Remove the algae in between the scales. Don’t use any type of soap unless your veterinarian has given you a prescription soap for a specific medical condition.

  5. Rinse your turtle with clean de-chlorinated water. You can pour it over your turtle with a pitcher.

  6. Throw away the toothbrush. You can buy another one the next time you have to bathe your turtle.

  7. Pour the water into the toilet and flush the toilet. Do not pour the water into the sink. Rinse the tub out and pour that water into the toilet, as well. Store the tub somewhere safe and wash your hands with soap.
