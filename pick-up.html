How to Pick Up Your Turtle

  1. Use two hands to pick up your turtle, with one hand on the left side of its body and one on the right side of its body. Hold onto the shell between the front and back legs and lift it slowly up.

  2. Never turn your turtle over quickly. You should take a full two seconds, at minimum, to turn your turtle if you need to looks at its underside. Don’t turn it from side-to-side; instead, turn it head over tail. When you turn it back over, reverse what you did, raising its head and slowly turning it onto its feet. Turning it quickly or from side-to-side puts stress on its organs and can lead to twisted intestines which can be fatal.

  3. Do not turn over your turtle if you don’t need to and don’t keep it on its back unnecessarily. This stresses out the turtle.

  4. Let your turtle walk on your hands. Be sure you are close to the ground or some other surface so he can’t fall.

  5. Don’t make any sudden movements with your hands while holding your turtle. All movements should be slow and steady.

  6. Place your pet back down carefully and slowly. Make sure it is put down on it feet only, never on his back.

  7. Never tap on its shell. This can stress your turtle and can even cause injury.

  8. Wash your hands with soap. Turtles carry salmonella and you need to be careful to wash your hands immediately after handling it.
