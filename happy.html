How to Tell if Your Turtle is Happy and Healthy

  1. Read a book about turtles and their care. There are many different species of turtles and they all have slightly different needs. Learn more about the type you have by reading a book about the species you have.

  2. Talk to a veterinarian who is experienced in the care of turtles. Ask any veterinarian that you consider if they regularly treat turtles. If they don’t, ask for a referral to one who does. You might need a different vet for your turtle than the one you use for your dog or cat, if applicable.

  3. Examine your turtle’s shell regularly. A turtle’s shell is made up of bony plates called scutes. The shell is often the site of any health problems she might have. Look for ulcers, soft scutes, scutes that look as though they are pyramid-shaped, and other deformities. These issues should be brought to the prompt attention of your veterinarian.

  4. Check your turtle’s skin. If you see a lot of flaking or any mites or parasites on its skin, make an appointment with your veterinarian.

  5. Check its eyes, nose, and mouth. If its eyelids are swollen or her nose is running, it may have a vitamin A deficiency. This is serious in turtles and warrants a prompt visit to the veterinarian. If it is breathing through its mouth or wheezing, this can be indicative of a respiratory infection.

  6. Watch its activity. Turtles vary in how active they are, but get to know what is normal for your pet. If it isn’t eating as well or is not swimming around as much as she usually does, there could be something wrong. Check with your veterinarian.
